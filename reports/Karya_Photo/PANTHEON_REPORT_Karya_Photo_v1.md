## 2. PTM SIGNAL ANALYSIS (Primary Target Market)

### ACTUAL PTM: The Legacy-Anxious Parent (Ages 42-44)

**Not the bride. The parent paying for their own psychological reassurance.**

**Three pure receptive profiles emerged:**

**Agent Marcus/42** (Temp 8/10, Intent: save): *"This speaks directly to who I am—someone who understands that what we choose to preserve defines our place in the world, and Karya's 40 years means they've already witnessed the dignity of families like mine."*

- **Psychology:** Actively grieving his own father's inadequate photographic archive. The wound is specific: "When my father died we didn't have a *good enough* photo." He is purchasing preventative immortality.
- **Financial signal:** Already calculating 25-35 juta cost and pre-justifying it as "investment in family legacy" to his wife.
- **Conversion trigger:** The fear of being "forgotten poorly."

**Focus Group Participant Chua/42**: *"My parents used them in the '80s. This campaign understands that."* Yet internally: *"My own sons probably think like Rizki—they'll want whatever looks good on Instagram, not what lasts. And I don't know how to bridge that gap without sounding old and irrelevant."*

- **Psychology:** Defending a world he knows is already obsolete. The campaign is his "last stand against irrelevance."
- **Key admission:** He didn't use Karya for his own wedding. This campaign is making him retroactively regret his past choices.

**Agent Age 42** (Female business owner, Temp 7/10, Intent: save): *"This isn't trying to sell me a photograph—it's trying to sell me the idea that I've already failed if I don't think about my children's inheritance, and that stings because it's true."*

- **Psychology:** The sting is proof of receptivity. She's converting shame into purchase justification.

**Focus Group Participant Darmanto/44**: *"My daughter is only fourteen, but this campaign is already making me feel like I've failed to think about her wedding as a legacy statement. Have I even built anything worth inheriting?"*

- **Psychology:** Pre-emptive anxiety for an event a decade away. The Fortuner will be worthless; the photograph becomes the inheritance substitute.
- **Financial vulnerability:** Spent 22 million on daughter's birthday party, now questioning if that was "obscene"—reveals wealth guilt seeking redemption through "dignified" spending.

### THE PTM FORMULA:
**Age 40-44 + Parental status + Specific mortality awareness + Wealth without legacy artifacts = Maximum receptivity**

The campaign doesn't sell to people who *have* legacy. It sells to people who *fear they haven't built one.*

## 6. THREE SHARPENING RECOMMENDATIONS

### RECOMMENDATION 1: **Create the "Daughter's Edit" as a parallel micro-campaign**

**EVIDENCE:**
- Agent Dewi/31: *"This is speaking to the part of me I don't admit to—the tension between what I actually believe and what I perform"*
- Agent Natalia/31: *"authenticity and inheritance aren't opposing forces, they're the same photograph seen twice"*
- Focus group Maya/31: *"My mother's wedding photos are Polaroids in a plastic album... they're precious to me. But this campaign would categorize them as... not legacy."*

**THE FIX:**
Produce 3-4 Instagram posts (not replacing the main 24, *supplementing*) that speak **directly to the bride without parental mediation**. Show a bride in private, looking at her grandmother's photo alone—no family present. Caption addressing her in first person: *"Before it becomes theirs to inherit, it has to be yours to live."*

**EXPECTED RESPONSE SHIFT:**
Converts the "wary recognition" segment (Temp 6-7, Intent: glance → save) by giving them permission to want this *for themselves* before performing it for family. Reduces "trapped recognition" temperature by 2 points.

### RECOMMENDATION 2: **Name the craft explicitly in at least 3 hero posts**

**EVIDENCE:**
- Dr. Melinda/38: *"There's no discussion of lighting technique or archival preservation or what actually makes a photograph last forty years."*
- Agent Age 38 (physician): *"I'm noticing they've done something unusual: they're not screaming at me like every other Medan brand."*

Like an architect's drawing notation.

**EXPECTED RESPONSE SHIFT:**
Converts the skeptical professional class (doctors, lawyers ages 31-38) who need *evidence* to justify emotional response. Raises perceived legitimacy without diluting emotional message. Turns "intellectual irritation mixed with professional respect" into "validated respect."
